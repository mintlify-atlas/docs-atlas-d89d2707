Special categories identify sources with particular characteristics that editors should be aware of when evaluating citations.

## AI-Generated

<Accordion title="About AI-Generated Content">
This source is associated with unedited AI-generated content. It may be a website that publishes substantial AI-generated material without human editorial oversight.

AI-generated content lacks human verification and fact-checking. These sources should generally be avoided unless the AI output has been thoroughly reviewed and verified by qualified humans.
</Accordion>

**Icon:** Pink/red AI symbol

## AI-Referred

<Accordion title="About AI-Referred Sources">
The URL of this source includes tracking parameters (such as utm_source=chatgpt.com) indicating it was copied from an AI assistant. The associated text may have also been AI-generated, and the source may or may not corroborate the given statements.

Sources with AI referral parameters suggest the citation may have come from an AI chatbot. Verify that the source actually supports the claim and wasn't hallucinated or misrepresented by the AI.
</Accordion>

**Icon:** Tan/beige AI symbol

## Advocacy

<Accordion title="About Advocacy Organizations">
This source is an advocacy organization.

Advocacy organizations promote specific causes or viewpoints. While they can be reliable for basic facts about themselves, use caution when citing them for controversial claims or opinions on topics they advocate for.
</Accordion>

**Icon:** Advocacy/megaphone symbol

## Editable

<Accordion title="About Editable Sources">
This source is editable by the community (e.g., a wiki or database).

Editable sources like wikis can be changed by anyone and lack stable, verified content. Wikipedia generally cannot cite other wikis or user-editable databases as reliable sources.
</Accordion>

**Icon:** Edit/pencil symbol with community aspect

## Government

<Accordion title="About Government Sources">
This source is identified as a state-owned or state-run media, or a government source.

Government sources are reliable for basic factual information about government activities but may have bias on politically sensitive topics. State-run media reliability varies by country and topic.
</Accordion>

**Icon:** Government building symbol

## Predatory

<Accordion title="About Predatory Journals">
This source is from a predatory journal.

Predatory journals charge publication fees without providing proper peer review or editorial oversight. They should not be used as reliable sources for Wikipedia content.
</Accordion>

**Icon:** Red predatory/warning symbol

## Satire

<Accordion title="About Satirical Sources">
This source publishes satirical or parody content.

Satirical sources intentionally publish false or exaggerated content for humor or social commentary. They should not be used as factual sources but can be cited when discussing the satire itself.
</Accordion>

**Icon:** Comedy/satire mask symbol

## Sponsored

<Accordion title="About Sponsored Content">
This source is a sponsored content or promotional material.

Sponsored content is paid advertising designed to look like editorial content. It lacks editorial independence and should not be used as a reliable source.
</Accordion>

**Icon:** Dollar sign or sponsored symbol
